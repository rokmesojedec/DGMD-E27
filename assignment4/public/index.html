👨‍💻Design Concept

Posted: March 31, 2021 by Rok Mesojedec

The requirements for DGMD E27 Assignment 1 state that the submitted website needs to implement a grid/flex based layout. I decided that the visual design should reflect the spirit of grid/flex techniques and produce a play of rectangular geometric shapes with a pleasing and complementing combination of colors.

Having rectangles and angular shapes in mind, I tried experimenting with a misprint style, which can be an artifact of misaligned color layers when printing paper media. I tried to replicate this with each grid data cell, which has a colored border, with a slightly misaligned background, which protrudes outside the bounds of the border container by being slightly rotated by 1.5 degrees. The content inside the inner container is, in turn, rotated back to 0-degree horizontal alignment, to avoid affecting the readability of the content.

In addition to main article containers, I also styled site headings, by applying on them sharp text shadows which are colored in all the different color accents I picked for the color scheme of this site. This gives the heading an eye-catching and - in my opinion - aesthetically pleasing look. And what's really cool is that this is achieved with a code-only approach so no photoshop was needed.

🌕Rok's Awesome Gradient Mixin

This mixin takes three arguments. The first one $degrees is the amount of rotation in degrees applied to the generated gradient. $lighten must be a precentage value which lightens the passed colors. $colors must be a SASS list of valid color values.

@mixin gradient($degrees, $lighten: 0%, $colors){ background: nth($colors, 1); $colorValues: (); @each $color in $colors { $colorValues: append($colorValues, lighten($color, $lighten), comma); } background: linear-gradient($degrees, $colorValues); }
